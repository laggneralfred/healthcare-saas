# Facebook Discovery Questions

Purpose: learn from practitioners before pitching Practiq.

## General

- What part of practice software slows you down the most during a normal clinic day?
- Do your notes feel more like clinical thinking or data entry?
- If you could simplify one part of scheduling, notes, reminders, intake, or checkout, what would it be?
- Do you prefer natural Visit Notes, SOAP notes, or a mix depending on the visit?

## Acupuncture

- For acupuncturists: what do you wish your note software handled better around points, techniques, herbs, and follow-up?
- Do you document in a structured format, free text, or both?

### Ready-to-Post: Acupuncture

Documentation / admin pain:

- For acupuncturists: when you write treatment notes, what takes the most time: points, techniques, herbs, follow-up plans, or something else?
- Do your notes let you capture the actual clinical reasoning behind a treatment, or do they mostly feel like checking boxes after the visit?
- What part of your clinic admin tends to pile up by the end of the day: notes, reminders, intake forms, checkout, billing details, or patient messages?

AI comfort / trust:

- For acupuncturists using or considering AI: where would you actually trust it to help, and where would you want it nowhere near the clinical decision?
- Would AI-drafted note organization feel useful if you always reviewed and controlled the final note, or would that still feel uncomfortable?

Billing / reminders / import workflow:

- If you ever switched practice software, what made importing patient records difficult: point history, herbs, forms, billing data, or old note formats?
- How do you currently keep track of follow-up reminders, treatment plans, and patient instructions between visits?

Soft Practiq mention:

- I am building Practiq as a former practicing acupuncturist and longtime software developer, and I am trying to understand where acupuncture notes should stay natural instead of feeling like generic EHR fields. What would you want a calmer practice system to understand about acupuncture workflow?

## Massage Therapy

- For massage therapists: what do you usually need to remember between sessions that software does not capture well?
- Do intake and consent forms feel connected to your treatment notes, or separate?

### Ready-to-Post: Massage Therapy

Documentation / admin pain:

- For massage therapists: what do you wish your session notes captured better: areas worked, pressure, client preferences, contraindications, goals, or response to treatment?
- Do your intake forms, consent details, and session notes feel connected, or are you re-reading and re-entering the same information in different places?
- What admin task tends to interrupt the flow of your day the most: notes, scheduling, reminders, payments, forms, or follow-up messages?

AI comfort / trust:

- Would AI be useful for organizing rough session notes into a cleaner format if you stayed fully in control of what gets saved?
- Where would you draw the line with AI in a massage practice: note cleanup, client communication drafts, intake summaries, or none of the above?

Billing / reminders / import workflow:

- How do you currently handle package balances, visit reminders, and follow-up recommendations without adding more admin time?
- If you had to move your client records into a new system, what would worry you most: old notes, forms, appointments, payment history, or client preferences?

Soft Practiq mention:

- I am working on Practiq, a calmer practice system for independent practitioners, and I am trying to learn how massage therapists actually document care between sessions. What would make software feel more useful and less like extra work?

## Chiropractic

- For chiro and physio practitioners: where does SOAP documentation help, and where does it slow you down?
- How do you currently handle follow-up instructions and reminders?

### Ready-to-Post: Chiropractic

Documentation / admin pain:

- For chiropractors: where does SOAP documentation help your workflow, and where does it start feeling like admin for admin's sake?
- What takes longer than it should in your notes: exam findings, adjustments, care plans, outcome measures, billing support, or patient instructions?
- Do your systems make it easy to see the story of a patient's progress over time, or do you have to piece it together visit by visit?

AI comfort / trust:

- Would you trust AI to help organize chiropractic visit notes if it never finalized anything without your review?
- What would make AI feel useful but not intrusive in a chiropractic workflow: summarizing visits, drafting patient instructions, spotting missing fields, or something else?

Billing / reminders / import workflow:

- How much of your documentation is shaped by billing or compliance needs rather than the way you naturally think through a visit?
- If you changed systems, what would be hardest to preserve: care plans, SOAP notes, billing history, imaging references, reminders, or patient communications?

Soft Practiq mention:

- I am building Practiq for small practices in active development, and I am trying to understand where chiropractic software feels too rigid. What would a practitioner-shaped system need to get right before you would take it seriously?

## Physiotherapy

### Ready-to-Post: Physiotherapy

Documentation / admin pain:

- For physiotherapists: what part of documenting a normal visit takes the most time: assessment, exercises, progress measures, goals, plan updates, or patient education?
- Do your notes help you think clinically, or do they mostly serve reporting, billing, and compliance requirements?
- How do you keep home exercise instructions, reminders, progress notes, and patient communication connected without duplicating work?

AI comfort / trust:

- Would AI-assisted drafting be useful for summarizing progress and organizing notes, as long as you reviewed everything before it became part of the chart?
- In physiotherapy, where would you be comfortable using AI support: documentation cleanup, exercise instruction drafts, visit summaries, admin messages, or not at all?

Billing / reminders / import workflow:

- What usually creates the most friction around billing support or claims documentation: missing details, duplicated forms, diagnosis codes, treatment plans, or visit frequency?
- If you moved to a new practice system, what data would have to come across cleanly for you to trust the switch?

Soft Practiq mention:

- I am shaping Practiq around real practitioner workflows, and physio documentation has its own mix of clinical thinking, progress tracking, and admin requirements. What would make a system feel genuinely helpful rather than just another place to type?

## Wellness / Integrative Care

### Ready-to-Post: Wellness / Integrative Care

Documentation / admin pain:

- For wellness and integrative care practitioners: does your software fit the way you actually work, or does it force your visits into a medical model that does not quite match?
- What do you need to remember between sessions that typical booking or note tools do not capture well?
- Which admin pieces feel most disconnected in your practice: intake, goals, session notes, reminders, checkout, packages, or client communication?

AI comfort / trust:

- Would AI feel helpful for organizing client notes and follow-up drafts if you controlled the final wording and what gets saved?
- What would make AI feel trustworthy in a wellness or integrative care setting: transparency, review before saving, limited use, or avoiding clinical suggestions entirely?

Billing / reminders / import workflow:

- How do you currently manage package visits, reminders, follow-ups, and client history without scattering information across several tools?
- If you switched systems, what would you worry about losing: intake history, goals, session notes, payments, packages, reminders, or client messages?

Soft Practiq mention:

- I am building Practiq as a calmer system for small healthcare and wellness practices, and I am trying to learn where integrative workflows do not fit standard EHR or booking software. What should a practitioner-shaped tool respect from day one?

## Import / Switching Systems

- Has importing patient records ever stopped you from switching software?
- What format is your patient data currently in: spreadsheet, old EHR, paper, or mixed?
