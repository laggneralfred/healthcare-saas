## Weekly Review Checklist

- Which audience segment responded most clearly this week?
- Which post topic produced comments, DMs, demo interest, or interview interest?
- Which CTA worked best: question, DM, Explore Demo, Join Early Access, or interview invite?
- What exact practitioner language should be reused in messaging?
- What objections showed up more than once?
- Did AI messaging create trust, confusion, or resistance?
- Did any discipline need more specific language than the current positioning provides?
- What should be repeated, revised, or paused next week?

## If a Post Gets No Response

- Do not assume the pain point is wrong from one quiet post.
- Check whether the question was too broad, too polished, or too close to a pitch.
- Rewrite it as a more specific practitioner-to-practitioner question.
- Try a discipline-specific version before abandoning the topic.
- Remove product language unless the post was already warm or contextual.
- Log the result as "no signal" rather than "failed."

## If a Post Gets Comments But No Demo Interest

- Treat the comments as research first.
- Reply with curiosity and ask one clarifying question before offering a demo.
- Look for repeated phrases that can sharpen positioning.
- If the thread stays active, use a soft transition: "I am building Practiq around this kind of workflow, and your example is helpful."
- Offer Explore Demo only after the person shows interest in a better workflow, not just the problem.
- Log whether the barrier was timing, trust, unclear product fit, AI concern, price concern, or no stated need to switch.

## If a Post Gets Demo Interest But No Early-Access Signup

- Follow up once with a practical feedback question, not a sales push.
- Ask what would need to be true before they would use Practiq in their own practice.
- Identify whether the gap is missing features, trust, switching/import anxiety, pricing, workflow fit, or timing.
- Offer a short interview if the feedback is detailed.
- Reuse the objection in future posts or outreach copy.
- Log the decision as either "needs stronger proof," "needs feature clarity," "needs import reassurance," "needs pricing clarity," or "not a current fit."
