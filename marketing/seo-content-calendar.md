# Practiq SEO Content Calendar (24 Weeks)

## 1. Purpose of the Calendar

This calendar gives Practiq a steady, practical publishing plan focused on small-clinic owners and practitioners.  
The goal is to earn trust through genuinely useful articles, improve organic discovery, and support buying decisions without hype.

## 2. Publishing Rhythm

- Publish one article per week for 24 weeks.
- Keep articles evergreen and practical.
- Prioritize clarity over volume.
- Refresh internal links on older articles every 4-6 weeks.

## 3. Article Style Rules

- Write for humans first, search engines second.
- Use essay-like, practical guidance in plain language.
- Keep tone warm, professional, and grounded in small-clinic reality.
- Use real workflow examples and tradeoffs.
- Keep Practiq mentions light and natural near the end when relevant.
- Avoid keyword stuffing and template-like intros.

## 5. Internal Linking Rules

- Every article should link back to `/blog`.
- Every article should include 2-4 relevant links to existing public pages (practitioner pages, related blog posts, homepage, register).
- Use descriptive anchor text (not “click here”).
- Link only to published pages and existing routes.
- Keep links natural; avoid over-linking every paragraph.

## 6. SEO Publishing Checklist

Before publishing each article:

- Confirm route/view/slug exists and matches article title.
- Add/update sitemap URL entry in the direct XML list in `routes/web.php`.
- Confirm page is indexable (no `robots-noindex` include).
- Confirm `<title>`, meta description, one clear H1.
- Confirm GA partial inclusion follows existing public page pattern.
- Add at least 2 internal links and one contextual CTA link.
- Confirm claims are accurate to current product behavior.
- Run basic QA pass for readability on desktop/mobile.

## 7. Safety / Claims Guardrails

- Do not provide legal, tax, billing, or clinical advice.
- Do not promise reimbursement, payer acceptance, or financial outcomes.
- Do not claim Practiq is a clearinghouse, full accounting system, or autonomous booking engine.
- Do not claim Practiq provides diagnosis or replaces practitioner judgment.
- For AI content, state clearly that AI output is draft/support only and practitioner-reviewed.
- Use careful language: “may,” “often,” “many clinics,” “requirements vary.”
