# 14-Day Founder-Led Marketing Plan

Purpose: run a calm, practical 14-day learning sprint for Practiq using founder-led Facebook and group outreach.

Positioning to keep consistent:

- Practiq is a calmer, smarter practice system for independent healthcare practitioners.
- Less admin. Better notes. Smarter workflows.
- Built by a former practicing acupuncturist and longtime software developer.
- Early access is practitioner-shaped and in active development.
- Use Explore Demo and Join Early Access only when the conversation has earned it.

## 30-Minute Daily Routine

- 5 minutes: choose the day's audience and post from this plan.
- 5 minutes: lightly adapt wording to the specific group, removing anything that feels too polished or pitch-like.
- 5 minutes: post once in one relevant group or channel.
- 10 minutes: reply to comments with curiosity, ask one clarifying question, and avoid jumping straight to the demo.
- 5 minutes: record results in `experiments/facebook-response-tracker.md`.

## When to Invite

Invite someone to Explore Demo when:

- They describe a workflow pain that Practiq directly addresses.
- They ask whether you are building something for this.
- They ask to see the product.
- They compare current software negatively and sound open to alternatives.

Use: `outreach/early-access-email.md` > `Demo Invite`.

Invite someone to an interview when:

- They give a detailed workflow example.
- They mention a discipline-specific issue that is not yet clearly understood.
- They raise an objection about AI, switching, import, reminders, billing, or documentation.
- Their language sounds useful enough to shape positioning or roadmap decisions.

Use: `outreach/early-access-email.md` > `Interview Invite` or `outreach/follow-up-messages.md` > `DM After a Useful Facebook Comment`.

Invite someone to Join Early Access when:

- They have explored the demo and say it feels relevant.
- They ask how to try it.
- They express clear interest in shaping what gets built next.
- They identify a current workflow gap Practiq can reasonably support.

Use: `outreach/early-access-email.md` > `Invite to Join Early Access`.

## Rules for Replies

- Reply first as a practitioner listening, not as a vendor.
- Ask one clarifying question before offering Explore Demo unless they directly ask to see the product.
- Keep Practiq mentions soft: "I am building Practiq around this kind of workflow, so this is helpful."
- Use the founder line only when context helps: "former practicing acupuncturist and longtime software developer."
- Do not argue with objections about AI, switching, price, or workflow fit.
- Do not ask someone to Join Early Access before they understand what Practiq is.
- Keep DMs short and connected to something specific they said.
- Stop after one no-response follow-up.

## Weekly Review Checklist

Run this after day 7 and day 14:

- Which audience produced the most useful comments?
- Which post created the clearest practitioner language?
- Which topic led to DMs, demo interest, or interview interest?
- Which CTA worked best: clarifying question, DM, Explore Demo, Join Early Access, or interview invite?
- Did "calmer, smarter practice system" feel natural in conversation?
- Did "Less admin. Better notes. Smarter workflows." match the pains people described?
- Did the founder positioning increase trust or make the post feel too self-referential?
- Which objections repeated around AI, switching, import, billing, reminders, or pricing?
- Which post should be repeated with sharper wording?
- Which audience should get more attention next week?

## Avoiding Salesy Behavior

- Lead with a real question, not a product claim.
- Mention Practiq only when it gives context or someone shows interest.
- Use Explore Demo as an invitation, not a conversion push.
- Use Join Early Access only after the person has enough context to decide.
- Do not over-explain features in public threads.
- Do not correct practitioners' workflows.
- Do not imply AI replaces judgment.
- Do not use urgency, scarcity, launch hype, or exaggerated claims.
- Prefer "your feedback helps shape the roadmap" over "sign up now."
- Treat quiet posts as learning, not as a reason to push harder.

## Suggested Success Metrics

This stage is about signal quality, not volume.

- 14 posts or adapted posts published.
- 20 or more substantive practitioner comments.
- 8 or more useful DMs.
- 5 or more interview invitations sent.
- 3 or more interviews booked.
- 5 or more Explore Demo invitations sent to relevant practitioners.
- 2 or more Join Early Access invitations sent after demo interest.
- 20 or more exact practitioner phrases captured.
- 5 or more objections logged.
- 3 clear positioning learnings by day 14.
- 1 audience segment identified as the best next focus.
