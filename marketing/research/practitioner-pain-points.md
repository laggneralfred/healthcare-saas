# Practitioner Pain Points

## Notes

- Notes feel like data entry.
- Too many fields for simple visits.
- SOAP notes are useful sometimes but too rigid for every visit.
- Slow typing or dictation needs a flexible writing surface.
- Handwritten note transcription still exists in real workflows.

## Scheduling

- Calendar tools are often disconnected from clinical records.
- Walk-ins and retroactive visits need support.
- Appointments should help workflow but not be mandatory.

## Intake and Consent

- Forms often live separately from the patient chart.
- Paper forms create retyping work.
- Consent tracking can be inconsistent.

## Communication

- Practitioners repeat the same reminder and follow-up messages.
- Follow-up instructions are easy to forget.
- Patient emails need to be professional but not time-consuming.

## Import and Switching

- Data migration is scary.
- CSV imports require cleanup.
- Old systems may export messy data.
- Practitioners worry about losing access to records.

## Checkout and Billing Support

- Checkout can be disconnected from visits.
- Receipts, service fees, and product sales need simple workflows.
- Insurance-related documentation needs structure without making the whole system billing-first.
