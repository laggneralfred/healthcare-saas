# Practitioner Interview Script

Goal: understand daily workflow pain points and evaluate fit for early access.

## Opening

Thanks for taking the time. I&rsquo;m building Practiq, a calmer practice system for independent healthcare practitioners. I&rsquo;m especially interested in how your current workflow actually works, not just what software says it supports.

## Background

- What kind of practice do you run?
- Are you solo or part of a small clinic?
- What software or systems do you use today?
- What still happens on paper, spreadsheets, text messages, or memory?

## Workflow

- Walk me through a typical patient visit from scheduling to checkout.
- Where do notes fit into that flow?
- Do you use natural notes, SOAP notes, templates, or something else?
- How do you handle reminders and follow-up communication?
- How do intake and consent forms reach the patient record?

## Pain Points

- What feels slower than it should?
- What do you avoid doing because the software makes it annoying?
- What information is hard to find later?
- What would make switching software difficult?

## Practiq Fit

- Would a calmer system for records, scheduling, Visit Notes, reminders, emails, intake, checkout, import, and export be useful?
- Which feature would matter first?
- What would make you trust or distrust an early-access product?

## Close

Thanks. If Practiq seems relevant, I can send the sample-data demo and early-access link. I&rsquo;d also appreciate any feedback on what feels clear or unclear.
